    Visa and Work Authorization

    Understand the visa and work authorization processes to ensure a smooth transition into the professional world.


      1.Understanding Visa Types:


      • Familiarize yourself with different visa categories (F-1, OPT, H-1B, etc.).
      • Know the specific regulations and limitations of each visa type.
      • Consult with your university's international student office for guidance.

      • 2.Applying for Optional Practical Training (OPT):


      • Learn about OPT eligibility requirements and application procedures.
      • Submit required documents, including Form I-765, to the U.S. Citizenship and Immigration Services (USCIS).
      • Understand the OPT employment rules and reporting responsibilities.

      • 3.Navigating H-1B Visa Process:


      • Explore the H-1B visa application timeline and lottery system.
      • Understand the employer's role and responsibilities in the H-1B sponsorship process.
      • Ensure compliance with H-1B regulations during your employment.

      • 4.Work Authorization Duration:


      • Be aware of the duration of your work authorization under different visa categories.
      • Plan strategically for your career progression considering visa expiration dates.
      • Explore options for visa extensions or transitioning to other visa categories.

      • 5.Impact of Visa Status on Job Search:


      • Communicate openly with employers about your visa status and work authorization.
      • Understand how visa-related timelines may impact job application processes.
      • Seek legal advice or consult your university's international student office for specific concerns.

      • 6.Permanent Residency (Green Card) Considerations:


      • Explore the possibility of transitioning from a temporary visa to permanent residency.
      • Understand the various paths to obtaining a Green Card.
      • Consider consulting with an immigration attorney for personalized guidance.
